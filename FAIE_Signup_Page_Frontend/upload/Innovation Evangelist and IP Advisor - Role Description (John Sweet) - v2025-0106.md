**Innovation Evangelist and IP Advisor (IEIA): A Holistic Approach to
Faculty Success**

At Oregon State University (OSU), my role transcends the conventional
boundaries of a \"technology licensing manager.\" While the latter often
connotes a narrowly transactional focus on patent filings and licensing
deals, my position as an *[Innovation Evangelist and IP Advisor
(IEIA)]{.mark}* reflects a broader and more strategic commitment to
fostering faculty success across multiple dimensions.

My primary mission is to empower faculty by offering **holistic,
mission-aligned guidance** on intellectual property (IP) strategy,
innovation commercialization, and research dissemination.

Key aspects of my role include:

1.  **Balancing Academic and Commercial Goals**: I counsel faculty to
    achieve their aspirations in commercialization without compromising
    the academic mission of publishing. By navigating the delicate
    interplay between securing IP rights and ensuring timely
    publication, I help faculty maintain their scholarly impact while
    pursuing entrepreneurial opportunities.

2.  **A Business-Oriented Perspective Beyond Patents**: Unlike a
    traditional patent attorney or technology licensing officer, my
    approach integrates **business strategy** into IP management. I
    guide faculty through the broader innovation ecosystem, offering
    insights into market alignment, commercialization pathways, and
    startup development. This ensures that innovations are not just
    protected but positioned for real-world success.

3.  **Customized Faculty Support**: I tailor my advice to each faculty
    member's unique goals, whether they are looking to secure funding,
    license their technologies, launch a startup, or simply maximize the
    societal impact of their research. My guidance is rooted in
    understanding their aspirations, disciplines, and the potential of
    their work.

4.  **Simplifying Complex Concepts:** My role requires the ability to
    distill and communicate intricate legal and technical concepts --
    such as patent specifications and patent eligibility -- into
    straightforward language. This ensures that faculty members,
    regardless of their familiarity with IP law, can make informed
    decisions about protecting and commercializing their innovations.

5.  **Catalyzing a Culture of Innovation**: As an *evangelist*, I
    champion a culture where innovation thrives. This involves not just
    supporting individual projects but also contributing to broader
    institutional goals---connecting faculty with resources, building
    partnerships, and promoting an environment that values
    entrepreneurship and impact.

6.  **Technical-Functional Expertise in IP and Agreements**: In my
    technical capacity as a *technology licensing manager*, I help
    faculty:

    - Secure intellectual property rights in the domains of copyright,
      trademark, and patent law, ensuring their innovations and creative
      works are properly protected.

    - Negotiate a wide variety of agreements, including those with
      sponsors, commercialization partners, and other entities, to
      create pathways for funding, collaboration, and market impact.

Ultimately, my role is about enabling faculty to succeed in every domain
they value, ensuring their research reaches its full potential to
benefit society, the university, and their own professional growth.
